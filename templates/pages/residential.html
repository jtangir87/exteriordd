Asphalt Shingle

Asphalt shingle are the most popular roof found on more than 80% of all homes. Composite shingles use an organic or fiberglas base saturated with asphalt and the exposed surface is impregnated with small chips or reflective elements.

Shake

Shakes are made from thin, wedge-shaped pieces of natural wood. The most common wood shakes are cedar and yellow pine. Wood shake roofs are very visually appealing and have a longer lifespan than traditional asphalt shingles.

Metal

Metal roofs are virtually maintenance free and extremely durable. The most common metals used are steel and aluminum but copper and zinc are also options. These roofs are install by laying large steel panels on the roof deck with the seams overlapping in raised ridges that run vertically along the roof slope
